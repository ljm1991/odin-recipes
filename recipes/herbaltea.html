Herbal Tea to Ease Dry Cough

Description

This DIY tea blend is gentle yet powerful. Be aware that it is not a quick fix. It is designed to fortify your body over time with vitamins and nutrients to change the way you feel physically and emotionally.

The tea is made of four herbs and one secret ingredient:

The Herbs

  • Linden has been used to ease colds and infection. Linden ontains mucilages that create a protective coating that helps reduce the irriation of a dry cough.
  • Thyme contains essential oils that may ease a dry cough. It is packed with vitamin C and A to help strengthen the immune system.
  • Calendula is known to relieve inflammation and gain ground over lingering infections.
  • Marjoram has been used to ease colds, dry, coughs, and swollen noses for centuries, though there's not a lot of clinical evidence for why or how this works.

The Secret

An Onion! I know what you're thinking but yes, an onion. Besides its use for cooking, onion possesses strong anti-inflammatory properities that help ease infections. It also contains vitamins A, B2, C, and valuable minerals such as iron, potassium, and calcium.

Onion in tea alleviates nasal congestion, eases breathing due to congestion in the chest, reduces fever, and eases the pain of a cough.

Ingredients

  • 1 handful of Marjoram
  • 1 handful of Calendula
  • 1/2 handful of Thyme
  • 1 handful of dried Linden blossoms
  • 1 onion
  • Honey (as needed)

Steps

  1. Peel and slice one onion.
  2. Gather dried linden flowers, calendula, thyme, and marjoram and mix them well in a jar. You can also store the dried mixture for later.
  3. Place half of a cut up onion and 1 handful of dried flower mixture in a teapot and slowly add boiling water.
  4. Cap the teapot and let it steep for 10 minutes.
  5. Strain the infusion through a fine sieve, squeeze out all the liquid.
  6. Serve with honey and drink three times a day.
